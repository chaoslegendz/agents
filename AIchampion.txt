every single conversation has these 2 little letters
lots of tech is popping up

how do we succeed, how do we not get left behind
we can buy all the latest AI tools

but how do we get people to use it and use it well

who will step up, who will help to be the guide

the answer is AI champion

This isn't a fancy new job title
Think of it more like a calling
A role that is picked up by someone who gets it, sees the potential, sees the spark and wants to share that reality to the rest of the team

Anatomy 
- more of a mindset and willingness to do stuff instead of a specific position or background
- becoming a catalyst for change from where you're sitting
- think of it as a human bridge:  AI potential, enterprise -- AI champion in the middle -- practical team usage. Champion helps to build that connection
- enterprise handles the governance and tooling layer, looking to everyone to handle the business and functional layer and our AI champions to be that bridge.
- enthusiastic, passionate, hands-on-doer, connector, communicator

what do they do?
- pinpoint team pain points where AI can help
- drive adoption to use AI tools
- share knowledge from experiments / experience
- align initiatives to ensure it solves real world problems
- gather feedback to improve AI use
- bridge gaps to connect AI capabilities to business processes
- in short, become evangelist, sharing what works from experience, getting feedback and ensuring its solving a real world problem


I like to think of it as the ripple effect

What's one pain point or first opportunity you see in your teams?

>> Adoption of AI tools, conduct workshops, demos, hackathons
