## SPEAKER NOTES (per slide)

---

### SLIDE 1 — What Is an AI Champion?

You may have heard the term AI Champion floating around. Before I explain what we're doing in our area specifically, let me ground everyone on what this role actually means — because it's not what most people assume.

An AI champion is not someone from a central AI team parachuting in with slide decks. They're someone from your world — someone who understands your work and sits close enough to the day-to-day that they can help you figure out where AI actually fits.

Their job is three things: advocate (help you understand what tools exist and why to use them), connect (get you to the right resources so you're not starting from scratch), and feed back (tell the program what's working and what's missing). That last one matters — it's how we improve together.

---

### SLIDE 2 — How This Fits the Bigger Picture

You may have also seen the enterprise SDLC AI Champion program — that's a real program, it's active, and it's doing good work. It's focused primarily on engineering and software development workflows, centered around GitHub Copilot.

We're not creating a competing program. What we're doing is extending that model into areas that engineering-focused champions don't naturally cover — finance, treasury, marketing, HR, ServiceNow, employee apps. The tools and library overlap. The context and use cases are ours.

Think of it as full-stack coverage. Enterprise handles the dev layer. We handle the business and functional layer. Together, that's the whole area.

---

### SLIDE 3 — What Are They Working On?

So what does a champion actually do on a Tuesday afternoon? A few things.

They help you spot where AI could realistically save you time — not in theory, but in your actual workflow. They answer the "I've heard about this, but how do I start?" questions in a low-pressure way. They connect you to prompts and configurations that are already built so you're not reinventing the wheel.

And importantly — they capture what's not working too. If there's a blocker, a gap, something the tools can't do yet — that comes back to us so we can escalate it or work around it.

Across our area, we're starting to see this look like: report summarization in Finance, intake support in HR, content drafting in Marketing, ticket categorization in ServiceNow. These aren't theoretical — these are real starting points.

---

### SLIDE 4 — Where to Find Out More

A few things are available to you right now.

The AI Accelerator Library has reusable prompts and configurations — don't start from a blank page. [Link here if you have it.]

The enterprise program runs office hours that are open across TD — I'll drop the link in the chat.

And we're building out our own area presence — [Teams channel / SharePoint / your channel here] is where we'll be posting updates, wins, and resources. Drop it in the chat when you have it up.

### SLIDE 5 — What's Next

Two asks before I hand it back.

First — if you're using AI tools and something is working well, share it. Drop it in the channel, tell your champion, bring it to office hours. The people around you will benefit, and it helps us build a much stronger program faster.

## FULL SCRIPT (stitched, ~7 min at comfortable pace)

---

"Alright — I want to spend a few minutes talking about something that I think is going to matter a lot to how our area navigates AI over the next year. We're calling them AI Champions, and I want to explain what that means, what they're actually doing, and how you can connect with them — or even become one.

Let's start with what an AI champion is — and more importantly, what they aren't.

They're not someone from a central AI team. They're not there to pitch you on a product. An AI champion is someone embedded in your world — on your team, or close to it — who understands the work you do well enough to help you figure out where AI actually fits in it. Their job is to advocate for the right tools, connect you to resources so you're not starting from scratch, and feed back to the program what's working and what's missing. That feedback loop is important — it's how we keep improving.

Now — some of you may have seen the enterprise SDLC AI Champion program. That's a real program, it's active, and it's doing great work. But it's focused on engineering and software development workflows, primarily GitHub Copilot. It doesn't fully reach into finance, treasury, marketing, HR, ServiceNow, employee apps — the functional areas that make up a huge part of this room.

We're not creating a competing program. We're extending the model into those areas. Same tools and library at the foundation — our context, our use cases, our priorities layered on top. Think of it as full-stack coverage: enterprise handles the dev layer, we handle the business and functional layer.

So what does a champion actually do? On a given week, they might be helping someone on the team spot a workflow that's ripe for automation. Or answering the 'I've heard about this but where do I even start?' questions — informally, no slide deck required. They're connecting people to prompts and configurations that are already built, so you're not reinventing the wheel. And they're capturing blockers — things that don't work, gaps in the tools — and surfacing those back so we can address them.

Across our area, the early examples look like: summarizing reports in Finance, routing and triaging requests in HR, content drafting in Marketing, ticket categorization in ServiceNow. These aren't hypothetical — they're starting points we're already seeing traction on.

Where can you find out more right now? The AI Accelerator Library has reusable prompts and tool configurations — I'd start there. The enterprise program runs open office hours — I'll drop the link in chat. And we're building out our own area channel [link] where we'll share updates, wins, and resources as they come.

If you have a question about AI tools, your champion is your first call — that's the whole point.

Finally — two asks before I hand it back.

One: if something is working for you, share it. Drop it in the channel, tell your champion, bring it to office hours. What you're learning is exactly what someone else on this call needs.

Two: if you're interested in being a champion and your area doesn't have coverage yet — I want to hear from you. [Link or email.] We're not looking for experts. We're looking for people who are curious and willing to help their team navigate this.

Next milestone is [date] — our first cross-area champion session. More details coming.

Thanks — back to [boss's name]."
